World Best Lasagna

Lasagna is the quintessential Italian comfort food, a majestic dish of layered pasta, rich fillings, and savory sauces that satisfies on the deepest level. This classic preparation involves alternating sheets of pasta all'uovo (egg pasta) with a deeply flavorful ragù (often a meat-based sauce like Bolognese), creamy béchamel or ricotta, and generous amounts of mozzarella and Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese. The magic truly happens as the dish bakes; the sauces meld with the cheeses, softening the pasta into a tender, cohesive whole with an irresistible golden, bubbling crust.

More than just a meal, preparing and sharing a pan of lasagna is an act of love and tradition. While the construction may appear complex, the individual steps are straightforward, making it a perfect weekend project. Whether you stick to the classic lasagna alla Bolognese or venture into vegetarian options featuring spinach and mushroom, the result is an incredibly rewarding, hearty dish that promises delicious leftovers and is guaranteed to be a centerpiece at any family gathering or special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound sweet Italian sausage
  • ¾ pound lean ground beef
  • ½ cup minced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (6.5 ounce) cans canned tomato sauce
  • 2 (6 ounce) cans tomato paste
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 4 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley, divided
  • 1 ½ teaspoons dried basil leaves
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, divided, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon fennel seeds
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • ¾ pound mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese
